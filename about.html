About Me

UX Designer • Front-End Developer • Product-Focused Problem Solver

Hi, I’m Alanah Little, a Computer Science student at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte with a concentration in Human-Computer Interaction. I’m passionate about building digital experiences that are not only visually engaging, but also functional and intuitive. My interests span across front-end development, UX/UI design, product design, and software development, and I enjoy bringing ideas to life from both a design and technical perspective.

Through my coursework and projects, I’ve gained experience in wireframing, prototyping, usability-focused design, and front-end development using tools like Figma, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and GitHub. I enjoy thinking through how users interact with a product, while also understanding how those experiences are built and implemented. This allows me to approach problems with both a design mindset and a developer’s perspective.

I’m especially interested in opportunities that allow me to grow across multiple roles, including front-end developer, UX/UI designer, product designer, and software developer. I enjoy working across the full process, from understanding user needs and designing interfaces to building and refining them. I’m drawn to roles where I can combine creativity, logic, and problem-solving to create meaningful and seamless digital experiences.

Outside of academics and design, I value creativity, personal growth, and experiences that shape the way I see the world. My background and interests continue to influence my design style and perspective, allowing me to approach problems with curiosity, adaptability, and attention to detail.
