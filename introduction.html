Project SustAIn: Full Immersion Cooling Systems (Ethical Tech Innovation)

Course: ITSC 3688 - Computers and Their Impact on Society
Role: Lead Research Analyst
Timeline: January 2026 - Current

This project explored the ethical and environmental implications of computing energy consumption and introduced an innovative solution: Full Immersion Cooling using non-conductive mineral oil. My team designed and built a working small-scale prototype to demonstrate how computing systems can operate while fully submerged in cooling liquid, reducing heat and improving energy efficiency.

As part of the project, we presented our innovation in a Shark Tank–style pitch where we explained the environmental and technological challenges behind modern cooling systems, demonstrated our live immersion-cooled prototype, and evaluated the ethical and sustainability impact of our solution while proposing its potential for real-world implementation in greener computing infrastructure.

The Problem

Modern computing, especially AI and data centers, consumes enormous energy and produces significant heat, increasing environmental impact. Traditional air cooling and water cooling systems are energy-intensive and resource-heavy, raising ethical concerns about sustainability and responsible innovation.

The Innovation

Our team proposed Full Immersion Cooling Systems, where hardware operates fully submerged in non-conductive mineral oil, which:

  • Efficiently removes heat from CPUs.
  • Eliminates the need for traditional fans and heavy cooling infrastructure.
  • Reduces cooling energy consumption by up to ~85%.
  • Minimizes water usage compared to conventional cooling systems.
  • Demonstrates a scalable approach for greener computing.

We built a functional prototype using a Raspberry Pi system submerged in mineral oil to validate the concept. The system successfully ran while monitoring CPU temperature in real time, proving that electronics can operate safely in non-conductive liquid.

Prototype Highlights

  1. Small-scale immersion cooling system built with embedded hardware.
  2. Live CPU temperature monitoring through onboard display.
  3. Demonstrated heat reduction without traditional air cooling.
  4. Physical proof-of-concept for sustainable computing.

Ethical Impact

This project examined how technological innovation must balance performance and environmental responsibility. We analyzed:

  • Energy consumption of computing infrastructure
  • Environmental costs of cooling technologies
  • Sustainability vs. technological growth
  • Responsible design for long-term global impact

Our solution showed how engineering and ethical thinking can work together to reduce environmental harm while supporting high-performance computing.

Project: RideReady — Smart Transit Companion

Course: ITIS 3130 - Human-Centered Computing
Role: UX/UI Designer
Timeline: January 2025 - May 2025

RideReady is a mobile app prototype designed to help users easily schedule, manage, and stay on top of their car maintenance. The goal of this project was to create a simple and intuitive experience that reduces the stress of keeping up with vehicle service by allowing users to book appointments, track upcoming maintenance, and receive reminders in one organized place.

The interactive prototype was built in Figma and demonstrates a fully navigable mobile app experience, including core user flows, interface structure, and interaction design. The design focuses on clarity, usability, and helping users manage essential vehicle care without confusion or missed appointments.

Tools Used

  • Figma — Designed interface layouts, wireframes, and interactive prototype
  • Figma Prototyping — Created clickable navigation and user interaction flows
  • User Flow Mapping — Structured the app’s navigation and task paths
  • Wireframing — Planned layout, hierarchy, and screen organization
  • Interaction Design — Designed transitions, navigation behavior, and usability flow

Research and Insights

To understand the problem, I examined common frustrations users face when managing vehicle maintenance:

  1. Users forget upcoming appointments without reminders.
  2. Scheduling systems feel complicated or time-consuming.
  3. Maintenance history is often scattered or hard to track.
  4. Users want a simple, centralized place to manage vehicle care.

Design Goals

The core goals and values of RideReady were:

  • Make scheduling maintenance quick and simple.
  • Help users easily track upcoming appointments.
  • Provide clear and organized maintenance information.
  • Reduce cognitive load through intuitive navigation.
  • Create a calm, user-friendly mobile experience.

Key UX Skills Demonstrated

  • User-centered design thinking
  • Interaction design
  • Mobile app UX design
  • Information architecture
  • Wireframing and prototyping
  • Designing intuitive user flows
  • Simplifying complex user tasks

What I Learned

This project demonstrates my ability to design user-centered digital solutions that address real-world problems. RideReady shows how thoughtful UX design can simplify complex tasks, improve organization, and enhance everyday user experiences.

Through this project, I strengthened my skills in interaction design, mobile UX, and translating user needs into clear, functional interface design.
